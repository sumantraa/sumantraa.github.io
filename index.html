    Massachusetts Wellness Research Education Trust (501 c 3 non-profit)

    Mission

    To undertake actions free from desire and seek refuge in conduct performed with a proper attitude to promote 'Charitable' endeavors.

    Vision

    To conduct or support activities that promote affordable evidence-based health care to patients.

    To conduct or support efforts that promote higher education in the society.

    Design and implement high-quality biomedical research to find solutions for challenging diagnostic and therapeutic dilemmas in medicine.

    Services

    Adult Primary Care

    Comprehensive patient focused primary care evaluations

    Ambulatory Urgent Care

    Urgent evaluations for any non life threatening illnesses

    Ambulatory Cardiovascular Care

    Management of hypercholesterolemia, coronary artery disease, cardiac arrhythmias, congestive heart failure, blood clots and valvular heart disease. Electrocardiogram, genetic testing for heart attacks and home cardiac monitor testing (zio patch) can be performed.

    Insurance Participation

    We participate with most insurance companies in Massachusetts and also provide affordable direct pay options.

    Our Working Committees

    Affordable Health Care Committee (AHCC)

    The AHCC shall be responsible for and oversee any activities of the Trust as they relate specifically, but not limited, undertaking feasibility studies of health care delivery programs with focus on affordability and sustainability.

    AHCC may also support institutions focused on affordable health care delivery models and programs related to affordable prescription medicines.

    Phase I Activities (India)

    Innovation and Research Committee (IRC)

    The IRC shall be responsible and oversee activities that support biomedical research to find solutions in challenging human ailments.

    These activities may relate, but not limited to, supporting institutions or research groups focused on advancing disease diagnoses and therapies, evaluating research grant applications, and reviewing practical matters related to research standards, methods, and ethics.

    Phase I Activities (USA)

    Higher Education Committee (HEC)

    The HEC shall be responsible and oversee the Trust activities related to efforts promoting higher education in the public domain.

    Our Team

    Praveen Hariharan and his wife Kritika, with the support of their parents and mentors, founded the MAASHA Trust and the BHAASHA Foundation.

    For both the founding members, public service has been a part of their formal education and hope to continue this forward.

    Dr.Praveen Hariharan, MD, MPH, FACC

    Praveen Hariharan is a physician with clinical interests in adult internal medicine, preventive cardiology and venous thrombosis. His research interest include coronary artery disease and neurodegenerative disease. He is a Fellow of American College of Cardiology. He completed primary medical education from B.J. Medical College in 2008, post-graduate training in Internal Medicine from Boston University Medical Center/VA Boston Healthcare System with an additional clinical research fellowship from the Center of Vascular Emergencies at Massachusetts General Hospital and a Master's in Public Health from Boston University School of Public Health. He has held research and clinical appointments at multiple academic medical centers in Boston before founding the MAASHA Trust. In addition, he serves as an attending physician at the clinical decision unit at Brown University Health. In this capacity, he has served diverse underserved communities, including the veterans in Massachusetts, and mentored many post-graduate trainees. He has published original contributions in scientific journals and contributes to peer-reviewing for multiple scientific journals.

    Kritika Balasubramanian

    Kritika, is math teacher at Russian School of Mathematics. In addition, she is certified meditation teacher and a disciple of Sri Sri Ravi Shankar of the Art of Living foundation. She completed her basic computer science training in India before pursuing her post-graduation in computer science at Boston University.
